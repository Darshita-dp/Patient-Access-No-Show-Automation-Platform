# LinkedIn Post

I built a Patient Access Automation Platform to reduce no-shows and improve scheduling visibility.

After completing my Healthcare Revenue Cycle Command Center, I wanted to explore another major part of healthcare operations: patient access.

Missed appointments are not just a scheduling issue. They affect provider utilization, patient wait times, clinic capacity, and the overall care experience. So I built a system that helps scheduling teams identify high-risk appointments earlier and take action before the slot is lost.

For this project, I built an end-to-end platform with:

• A no-show prediction model using Python and Scikit-learn
• Risk categories for low, medium, and high-risk appointments
• A PostgreSQL data model for patients, appointments, providers, clinics, waitlists, reminders, and staff tasks
• A FastAPI backend to serve appointment, risk, waitlist, and utilization data
• A React frontend for scheduling teams to search appointments, review risk, and track recommended actions
• A waitlist matching engine to help fill open slots faster
• A Power BI reporting-layer design with documented DAX measures and executive dashboard mockups covering no-show rate, clinic utilization, provider schedules, waitlist demand, and staff action completion
• A Power Automate-style workflow specification and simulation for high-risk appointment outreach

The biggest lesson from this project was that prediction alone is not enough. A model becomes useful only when it connects to a real workflow.

That is why I focused on turning no-show risk scores into clear staff actions, waitlist recommendations, and manager-level visibility.

This project helped me strengthen my skills in healthcare analytics, SQL, Python, machine learning, Power BI, React, FastAPI, process mapping, and digital transformation.

I am continuing to build domain-specific healthcare technology projects that combine data, software, operations, and business problem-solving.
